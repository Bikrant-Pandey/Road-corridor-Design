# Road-corridor-Design
This project involved the complete civil design of a 2.278 km rural access road near Tokha,Kathmandu, Nepal using Civil 3D 2027. Real SRTM 30m elevation data was processed in QGIS and imported into Civil 3D to generate an existing ground surface. A horizontal alignment was designed following Nepal Department of Roads standards for 50 km/h design speed, with a typical cross section comprising 3.5m travel lanes, 1.0m shoulders and cut/fill slopes of 4:1 and 4:1 respectively. A 3D corridor model was generated along the full alignment, with earthwork volumes computed at 11,729.92 m³ cut and 19,836.12 m³ fill.To improve the cut/fill balance, the FG profile could be raised slightly in the valley sections to reduce fill requirements. However this would increase the maximum grade beyond the current design.  Final deliverables includs plan and profile sheets, cross section sheets and earthwork volume reports.
